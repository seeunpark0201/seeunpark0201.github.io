Street Smarts

IoT

Overview

Street Smarts is an IoT that collects data from street lights to monitor foot traffic and spark action to promote street safety.

Role

I was in charge of ideating, filming, and editing the video.

Skills

Storyboarding
Filming
Video editing

Team

Alia Friedman, Jisoo Geum, Amrita Sakhrani, Ben Stone

Duration

1 month

Prompt


How might we use IoT for civic engagement?


In order to narrow down our given prompt, we brainstormed different areas for civic engagement such as waste management, street safety, family health, etc. and came up with a scenario for each domain. We landed on street safety after voting on what we wanted to focus on. Below is an example of our brainstormed scenarios.





Problem Area

After doing some research regarding street safety, we decided to focus on finding a solution for these 3 main problems:


  1. Many people feel unsafe walking alone at night

  2. There are increasing numbers of pedestrian fatalities each year due to jaywalking

  3. Large numbers of homeless people who suffer from extreme weather conditions

This led to our problem statement:


How might we allow citizens to make informed decisions about navigating the city?


Ideation


We brainstormed different sensors and technology we could use to make an IoT that helps with street safety.




Insight


  • Streetlights are everywhere yet they are only used for light

Streetlights are prevalent in our lives yet they are not being used to the fullest potential. Just in the city of Pittsburgh alone, there are around 40,000 streetlights. Therefore, we came to a conclusion that streetlights can be equipped to sense pedestrian movement and the motion data collected can be used in various applications to increase pedestrian safety.


Solution


  • Streetlights collect data using motion sensors and monitors foot traffic
  • Heat sensors from streetlights can detect homeless people by calculating how long a person has been stagnant at a certain spot
  • Collected data can be used to reduce jaywalking by determining areas that need crosswalks
